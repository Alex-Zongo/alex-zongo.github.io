interactive · the reparameterization trick μ = (0.0, 0.0) · σ = (1.0, 1.0) · ρ = 0.0

x = μ + L·ε, ε ~ 𝒩(0, I), L Lᵀ = Σ

The same base noise ε (grey, a standard Gaussian) is deterministically reshaped into a sample x (colour). Sliding μ, σ, ρ translates and stretches the cloud — and because the map is differentiable, gradients can flow through it. That's the reparameterization trick: it's what lets us train models that sample.
